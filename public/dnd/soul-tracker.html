The Undercurrent
Field Observer's Guide · Classified — One Player Only

Something moves beneath the arena grounds. The earth trembles in irregular rhythms that don't match the crowd's weight. People near the outer districts report strange sounds — like something large swallowing. No one has seen what it is. No one has looked.

You are the only one who knows to look. Your role this session is to track, guide, and protect the thing moving underground — while staying invisible to the crowd, the officials, and the enforcers.

The Feeding Track

Each time you successfully complete a guidance action, mark one pip. Threshold milestones unlock something. You won't always know what.

Dormant Stirring Consuming Apex
The ground is quiet. You can feel it breathing.
Tremors increase. Something emerges briefly in the south district.
Three districts are emptying. Officials are confused. No one connects it to you.
The appetite is enormous now. What happens next changes the fight above.
Threshold Rewards
5 pips —The ground softens in one arena zone. Combatants above you choose: it becomes difficult terrain for enemies only, or one hidden passage opens into the arena floor.
10 pips —Something large and fed surfaces briefly near the outer wall. The crowd in that district scatters. Officials redirect. For two rounds, Enforcer attention is split — your allies in the arena have a window.
16 pips —The districts you guided have been quietly emptied. Their absence has weight — something important that was present in those seats is now gone. Pass a note to the DM: name one person from the crowd you made sure was NOT in the feeding path. They owe you something.
22 pips —The thing underground is enormous with what it has consumed. It can act once in the final confrontation — directly, visibly, decisively. The DM will tell you how. What it does will not be subtle.
Your Actions — Roll d10 Each Round You Act

On your turn, describe what your character does in the crowd or district area, then roll d10. The result modifies what happens — not always what you intended.

District Zones — Where to Point

The tournament grounds are divided into zones. You can only guide to zones you have line-of-sight to or have scouted previously. Each zone has different feeding density and risk.

The Outer Press
Densest crowd. Most undead among common attendees. High yield, high tremor visibility. Officials patrol the edge.
The Devotee Stands
Chimerakind faithful, packed tight. Medium yield. If tremors are noticed here, religious panic could cascade. High chaos risk.
The Noble Boxes
Low density, extraordinary quality. Each soul here counts double on the track. High Enforcer proximity. Very high detection risk.
The Underground Staging
Tunnels beneath the arena. Contestants, servants, handlers. Direct access to the underground path. No crowd to witness tremors.
The Outer Nations Camp
Foreign dignitaries and their retinues camped beyond the arena wall. Lowest detection risk. Slowest feeding — the path must travel farther.
The Dead Sections
Undead attendees congregate here by tradition. Feeding yields nothing — but leading the underground through here generates no tremors. Use as a transit corridor.
When Tremors Are Noticed
Officials and nearby crowd members who notice a tremor will investigate toward the last zone you guided. If questioned, your character has heard: "the old cistern system runs beneath the grounds — collapses occasionally during events of this size." This explanation works twice. On a third inquiry, officials call for a structural inspection. The underground must go dormant for one full round or risk exposure.
What You Are Not Told

"The tremors are hunger. The hunger has a name you have heard before, spoken gently in a different context. Whatever is moving beneath this arena came here for a reason that has nothing to do with the tournament. It is here because something it loves is in danger above. Guide it well."

Do not share this card. Do not read it aloud. Play what you feel.
