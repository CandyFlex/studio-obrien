The Story

Beautiful things should taste beautiful, too.

Maya Chen left a career in graphic design to go to pastry school in 2016. Her rationale was simple: she wanted to make things people could actually eat. Three years in Charlotte bakeries taught her the craft. A tiny storefront on Lafayette Street gave her a home for it.

She named it Butter + Bloom — butter because everything starts with good butter, bloom because flowers are her design inspiration. The name is also a promise: every cake should bloom with flavor.

"The croissants sell out by 9am. The custom cakes make people cry at weddings. This is what happens when a designer becomes a baker."

Today, Butter + Bloom is Shelby's destination for celebration cakes, delicate French macarons in rotating seasonal flavors, and butter croissants that people drive from Gastonia for. Maya still designs every custom cake herself — old habits from the design studio die hard.
