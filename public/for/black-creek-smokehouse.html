Our Story

Three Generations of Smoke

Eddie Ray Coleman built his first smoker out of a 55-gallon drum in 1987. He was 22 years old, working at the textile mill by day and selling barbecue out of his driveway on weekends. Word spread. By 1992, the line stretched down Buffalo Creek Road every Saturday.

His son Travis grew up tending the pit alongside him — learning which wood burns hottest, when to wrap, when to rest. Today, they run the Smokehouse together. Eddie Ray still lights the pit every morning at 3am. Travis runs the kitchen. His daughter Maddie handles the front of house.

"The recipes are the same ones Eddie Ray's mother wrote down on a notecard in 1965."

Every sauce, side, and dessert is made from scratch daily. The Coleman family has been smoking meat over hickory and oak for three generations. Pull up a chair. Stay a while.
