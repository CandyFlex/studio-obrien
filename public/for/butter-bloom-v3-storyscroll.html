Chapter 01

Designer to baker. Pixels to pastry.

Maya Chen left a career in graphic design to go to pastry school in 2016. She wanted to make things people could actually eat — things that brought joy, not just looked good on a screen.

Three years in Charlotte bakeries taught her the craft. A tiny storefront on Lafayette Street gave her a home for it. She named it Butter + Bloom: butter because everything starts with good butter, bloom because flowers are her design inspiration.

Chapter 02

A home on Lafayette Street.

The shop opened in 2019 — a warm space with a pink door, a display case full of color, and a kitchen where Maya works before sunrise.

Word spread fast. The croissants were selling out by 9am. The custom cakes were making people cry at weddings. Shelby had found its bakery.
