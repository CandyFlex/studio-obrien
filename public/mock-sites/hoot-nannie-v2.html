Where the Farmer Meets the Pitmaster, and 68 Bourbons Line the Wall

Forest City, N.C. // Staff Report
A sampling from the kitchen: farm-raised steaks, whole hog BBQ, and Brown Creek cheese curds.

Every plate at The Hoot Nannie starts on the farm and ends over flame. Johnny Roy started making barbecue sauces over 12 years ago. That sauce now sits on shelves at Food Lion and Ingles across North Carolina.

Good sauce needs good meat. Good meat needs good land. He partnered with Stephen "Neighbor Steve" Soto, combined the best of The Honey Hog and Nannie's Fish Camp, added farm-raised steaks from Beam Farms, and built a full bar with 68 bourbons.

The Hoot Nannie opened June 2021 on Main Street in Forest City. Behind every plate is Honey Hog Farm. 126 acres owned, 140 leased. Hogs, Wagyu cattle, chickens. All of it raised right here in Rutherford County.

The Bourbon Bar

Feature

Full bar with a bourbon wall that goes deep. Sixty-eight labels and counting line the shelves behind the bar, from familiar faces to small-batch bottles most people have never seen.

Craft cocktails mixed by people who care about the details. House-made watermelon margaritas when the weather turns warm. The bar stays open late on Fridays and Saturdays, with live local music filling the room.

"68 bourbons on the wall, and counting."

Sit at the bar. Wait for your steak. Let the bartender pour something you haven't tried yet. The drink menu gives you reasons to stay, and the staff knows every bottle on that wall.
