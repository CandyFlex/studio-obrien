Our Story

How We Got Here

From homemade hot sauce to a restaurant group with its own farm. Here is how The Hoot Nannie came to be.

2012
Johnny Ray's Signature Sauces

Johnny Ray Bousselot, originally from Texas, starts making hot sauces in North Carolina. Friends and family push him to go commercial. The brand launches out of Mount Holly, NC.

2015
Honey Hog Farm

Tyler Jones buys 40 acres in Lincoln County. The partnership with Johnny Ray begins, combining food and farming. They start raising their own hogs, Wagyu cattle, and chickens.

2018
The Honey Hog Restaurant

The first restaurant opens in Fallston, NC. BBQ and smokehouse fare sourced directly from their own farm. The farm-to-table concept takes root.

2021
The Hoot Nannie Opens

Johnny Ray and Stephen Soto combine the best of The Honey Hog and Nannie's Fish Camp. They add farm-raised steaks and a full bourbon bar, and open on Main Street in Forest City.

Our Story

The Farmer & The Pitmaster

The Hoot Nannie was born from a merger of two Fallston restaurants. Johnny Ray Bousselot and Stephen Soto combined the best of The Honey Hog, a BBQ smokehouse, and Nannie's Fish Camp, then added farm-raised steaks and a full liquor bar.

They opened on Main Street in Forest City in mid-2021, filling a space left vacant after COVID closures. The building is a faded brick structure on the corner of Main, and from the parking lot, you can smell the grill before you ever walk in.

"This is a farm to table type of place. Most of what you eat is sourced locally, and you can taste the difference."

Behind every plate is Honey Hog Farm. 126 acres owned, 140 more leased. Hogs, Wagyu cattle, and chickens. Steaks from Beam Farms in Rutherford County. Cheese curds from Brown Creek Creamery. Johnny Ray's signature sauces, born from over 12 years of experimenting, are now sold in grocery stores across the state.
