# Growth Strategy — post data-reframe (2026-07-20)

The forward plan, rebuilt on the evidence from the wide-net pull
(`research/data-reframe-2026-07-20.md`). Supersedes the old organic-first,
geo-page emphasis. Companion: SEO-STRATEGY.md (doctrine/ledger),
PIPELINE.md (build queue), POSITIONING.md (voice).

## The thesis in one paragraph

Organic search is not our near-term channel — 90 days of first-party GSC data
is 385 impressions and 2 clicks, and our domain authority can't crack page one
for anything competitive yet. The surface a new local studio CAN win is the
**Google map pack**, which ranks on proximity + relevance + reviews, not
authority — and we already have a claimed profile with 5.0/6 reviews sitting
under-optimized and absent from the packs. So the strategy is: **win the map
pack first (it drives calls), make everything we publish genuinely
non-derivative (the only kind of content that ranks or gets cited from a
low-authority site), and build demand off-search in parallel** so we're never
dependent on rankings we don't yet have.

## Pillar 1 — MAP PACK (primary; highest leverage, lowest effort)

**Why first:** it's the only surface where authority doesn't lock us out, the
searchers have hire intent, the competition is soft (Shelby review ceiling =
12; some #1s have 0 reviews), and we're already halfway in (claimed profile,
right category, 6 five-star reviews). It is contact-and-call driven — Jarred's
stated instinct — not a content bet.

**Build:**
1. **Complete the GBP** (Jarred owns; this is a settings fix, not a project):
   service area = Shelby + Cleveland County + Kings Mountain/Lincolnton/Gastonia,
   business hours, a description in the POSITIONING voice, the full services
   list, and 8-12 real photos (work, workspace, Jarred). This alone likely
   moves us from ABSENT into the packs — Google currently lacks the location
   signal to rank us.
2. **Review engine:** a repeatable habit — every finished client gets a
   personal review ask with a direct link. Target: pass 12 (the current pack
   ceiling) within a few months. Real reviews only.
3. **Measure:** `maps-track.mjs` weekly logs pack entry + competitor review
   growth. Pack entry is the first real outcome signal in the whole system.

## Pillar 2 — ONE non-derivative cost resource (secondary; optional, explained)

### What "pricing cluster" actually means
A **content cluster** is a group of interlinked pages on one topic — a central
"pillar" page plus supporting pages — that together tell Google we're a real
authority on that topic. A **pricing (cost) cluster** = a pillar answering the
biggest question every website buyer has, "how much does a website cost,"
supported by a few sub-answers, all interlinked and all feeding the true-cost
calculator we already built.

It is **not a price-list page.** We are not publishing rate cards. It's an
educational resource that captures the highest-intent researchers in the
market (Labs-verified demand: "how much does a website cost" 1,900/mo kd 8,
"website design cost" 1,600/mo kd 12, family ~3,000+/mo at kd 6-19) and routes
them to the calculator and to contact.

### Why it's secondary, not primary
Organic is our weak channel and Jarred is (rightly) cool on leading with
price. So this is **optional upside**, greenlit only if we can make it the
best cost resource in the market — otherwise it's derivative filler and we
skip it. The bar is the uniqueness doctrine below.

### If we build it, it's ONE pillar page, not a farm
`/what-a-website-actually-costs` (or similar): answer-first, FAQ schema for the
PAA set, calculator embedded, interlinked to the existing cost articles we
already have. Maybe 2 supporting posts later. Quality over cadence.

## THE UNIQUENESS DOCTRINE (the center of gravity)

The internet has ten thousand "how much does a website cost" articles, all
reworded from each other: "$500-$5,000 depending on complexity." Google and the
AI answer engines are drowning in it. A low-authority site publishing another
generic ranges-article is invisible AND adds nothing. **The only content worth
our time is content a competitor or a scraper physically cannot reproduce.**
Everything we publish must clear at least 3 of these non-derivative sources
(this IS RESEARCH-CHAIN Stage 4, applied):

1. **First-party project reality.** Jarred actually quotes and builds these.
   Real (anonymized, permission-based) breakdowns of what a real Shelby-area
   project actually cost and why — line items, trade-offs, what got cut. No
   competitor has Jarred's numbers. (HARD RULE: real data only, never
   fabricated — see the fabricated-proof purge. If we don't have a real
   number, we don't invent one.)
2. **The working calculator.** Most cost content is static prose; ours has a
   validated interactive true-cost tool. Interactive + owned = a genuine
   differentiator and an AEO asset (something to be cited FOR).
3. **Local market reality.** Not national ranges — "what a Cleveland County
   small business actually needs vs. what it gets upsold," real local context,
   real local examples (Ben's Smokehouse, the community spots), the honest
   version of the local market.
4. **Consumer-advocate voice (POSITIONING).** Written to genuinely protect the
   reader — "here's how the $99/mo builder actually costs you more," "here's
   what you truly need vs. what agencies pad the quote with." Teach, don't
   sell; never condescend. The derivative farms can't copy this because they're
   optimizing to sell, not to be trusted.
5. **Real teardown transparency.** Show an actual anonymized quote breakdown —
   the radical transparency nobody in the industry offers.

**The test for every piece before it's built:** list what it will contain that
does not already exist on page one or in the model. Fewer than 3 real entries
→ it doesn't ship. This is what makes us the *source* an AI cites rather than
one of the thousand pages it averages into mush.
