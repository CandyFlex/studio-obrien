# Step 2 — The Review Engine (get past the 12-review ceiling)

Reviews are the #1 lever we control in the map pack (proximity + relevance are
mostly fixed; prominence/reviews is where we win). Shelby pack ceiling = 12
reviews; we're at 6 with a 5.0. Getting to ~13-15 genuine 5-star reviews likely
puts us at/near the top of the Shelby pack. At even 1/week that's ~2 months.

**HARD RULE:** real reviews from real clients only. Never buy, fake, or
incentivize them (against Google policy + our no-fabrication guardrail).

## The habit (this is the whole game)
1. **Ask every client. No exceptions.** The #1 reason businesses have few
   reviews is they never ask.
2. **Time it to the moment of delight** — at launch, when they say "this looks
   great," when something you built works for them. Not weeks later.
3. **Make it frictionless** — send the direct link; never make them hunt.
4. **Personalize every ask** — their name + their project. Generic asks get ignored.
5. **One polite follow-up** if no response after ~5-7 days, then let it go.
6. **Respond to every review** you receive (a warm thank-you). Google rewards
   engagement and it shows you're active.
7. **Jump-start with PAST happy clients** — you don't have to wait for new ones.
   A few past clients (Ben's Smokehouse, community spots, prior clients) asked
   this week could move you toward the ceiling fast.

## Handling the edge cases
- **Negative/critical review:** rare if you ask happy clients. Respond calmly
  and professionally, thank them, offer to make it right offline. One good
  response to a bad review builds more trust than a wall of perfect ones.
- **No response:** one friendly nudge, then move on. Don't pester.
