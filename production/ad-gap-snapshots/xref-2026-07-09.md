## The four dimensions

- **DD** Directory Dominance - % of keywords grading WEAK (>=3 of top-5 organic are directories/aggregators). Higher = bigger opportunity to displace.
- **SA** Specialist Absence - 10 - mean(real local specialist count in top-10). Higher = thinner real competition.
- **DR** Demand Richness - mean(PAA + relatedQueries per keyword). Higher = more active search demand.
- **CNAF** Cross-Niche Advertiser Frequency - count of this niche's top-10 domains that also appear in 2+ niches' top-10. Higher = more multi-niche prospects (one pitch multiplies).

## Interpretation guidance

1. The matrix is the output, not a verdict. Pick the niche whose shape fits the play.
2. TOP-RIGHT quadrant niche = Route A applies with the specialist-absence gap read ("stop subsidizing Yelp").
3. TOP-LEFT quadrant niche = Route B1 archetype route (show what better looks like).
4. CNAF > 0 cross-niche domains get higher priority on the outreach list (one pitch multiplies value).
5. Filter by DD >= 0.30 if you want only niches with thick directory layer. Niches below DD 0.30 have a different gap shape (specialist-led, not directory-led).
6. DR is per-keyword density. A niche with low DR can still be a gap if SA is high - demand exists, just not in question-form yet.
