# Naming — options to pick through (systems, tracks, community program)

Ad-copy-style naming for the offering. Presented as coherent WORLDS (each a
consistent voice across system + 3 tracks + community) so the family holds
together — pick a world, or mix slots across worlds. Grab-bag alternatives
below for mixing. Voice per POSITIONING: plain, warm, confident, local; never
jargon, never cute-for-its-own-sake.

Replaces the weak internal label "à la carte." Candidates for the MODEL name:
**Growth Tracks · Build Your Plan · Your Plan, Your Pace · The Growth Stack.**

---

## WORLD 1 — "Plain Outcomes" (safest, most on-brand)
- **System:** The Local Growth Plan
- **Tracks:** Get Found · Get Growing · Get Known
- **Community:** Main Street Grant
- **Monthly:** Your Growth Plan ("we grow it and show you the proof")
- *Vibe:* exactly your positioning — plain, outcome-first, zero jargon. Lowest
  risk, instantly understood by any owner.

## WORLD 2 — "Roots & Growth" (memorable local metaphor)
- **System:** Rooted  (alt: Homegrown)
- **Tracks:** Roots (get found / foundation) · Branches (grow / reach out) ·
  Canopy (get known / everywhere)
- **Community:** Homegrown Grant  (alt: Planted)
- **Monthly:** Keep Growing
- *Vibe:* organic-growth story, warm, local, more distinctive/brandable. Risk:
  a touch softer, less literal about "Google."

## WORLD 3 — "Show Up" (punchy buyer verbs, ad energy)
- **System:** Show Up  (Studio O'Brien's growth system)
- **Tracks:** Show Up (found) · Stand Out (grow) · Get Chosen (known)
- **Community:** The Good Neighbor Grant
- **Monthly:** On the Rise  (alt: Stay Ahead)
- *Vibe:* confident, direct, ad-ready, framed entirely on the customer's win.
  Strong for graphics/headlines.

## WORLD 4 — "Foothills / Carolina Roots" (place-anchored)
- **System:** The Foothills Method  (alt: Cornerstone)
- **Tracks:** Cornerstone (found) · Momentum (grow) · Landmark (known)
- **Community:** Foothills Founders  (alt: The Cornerstone Grant)
- **Monthly:** Mainstay
- *Vibe:* local pride, established, craftsman feel. Cleveland County sits in the
  Blue Ridge foothills — real roots. Risk: less "growth-y."
