# Backlink & Off-Site Authority Strategy

The plan for earning links **from** other sites **to** studioobrien.com. This is
the "Rank Factor" multiplier (external validation) that on-page work can't buy.
Companion: `CONTENT-SYSTEM.md`, `ARTICLE-SPEC.md`, `SEO-AUDIT.md`.

## The strategic edge (from LocalIntel)

The 9 analyzed local competitors (Broad River Media, Jessica Leigh, Shield,
Studio29, Raven Media, EB Tech, The Image Adjusters…) share one weakness: **none
have a blog, schema, or a linkable asset.** They are static brochure sites. That
means they have *nothing worth linking to* — no data, no tools, no guides. Studio
O'Brien already has all three. The whole strategy: **be the only local web studio
that publishes link-worthy things, then get those things in front of people who
link.** Authority compounds because no competitor is competing for it.

## Rules (do no harm)

- **Never** buy links, use PBNs, or do reciprocal-link schemes. Google's link-spam
  systems penalize these; one bad vendor can undo a year of work.
- Relevance + locality beat volume. One link from the Cleveland County Chamber
  outweighs 50 directory scraps.
- Consistent **NAP** (Name, Address, Phone) everywhere. Mismatched citations
  actively hurt local ranking.

## Tier 1 — Local citations (fastest, do first)

Structured listings with consistent NAP. Mostly the owner's action; each is a
real inbound link + a local-relevance signal.

- **Google Business Profile** — the single most important. Complete every field,
  categories, services, weekly posts (ties to the GBP articles on the blog).
- **Bing Places, Apple Business Connect.**
- **Cleveland County / Shelby Chamber of Commerce** — member directory listing.
  Also Gaston, Lincoln, Rutherford, Catawba county chambers (the service area).
- **BBB** (accredited profile), **Yelp**, **Nextdoor** business page.
- Local/industry directories: Clutch, DesignRush, The Manifest, UpCity (web-design
  directories — competitors are absent from most).
- Data aggregators: Data Axle, Localeze, Foursquare (feed hundreds of smaller
  directories; fix NAP once at the source).

## Tier 2 — Linkable assets (already built or cheap to build)

Things other sites *want* to reference. Studio O'Brien has these; competitors don't.

- **The Website Cost Calculator** (`/website-cost-calculator`) — a genuine tool.
  Pitch it to local business groups, "resources for small businesses" pages, and
  Reddit/forums as a free no-email estimator.
- **Original-data blog posts** — the pieces citing real stats (GBP ranking factors,
  ADA lawsuit data, review velocity). Journalists and bloggers link to data. Turn
  the strongest into a shareable stat/graphic.
- **A local data study** (new, high-leverage): e.g., "We audited 50 Cleveland
  County small-business websites — here's what we found" (speed, mobile, GBP
  completeness). Local-news linkable; nobody else has the data.

## Tier 3 — Relationships & local PR

- **Local news:** Shelby Star, local radio, county business journals. Angle: the
  data study above, or "local web designer's take on [small-business trend]."
- **Partners/vendors:** photographers, copywriters, printers, marketing folks you
  work with — reciprocal *referrals* (not link schemes): partner pages, "who we
  work with" listings.
- **Clients:** every site you build should link back (footer "Site by Studio
  O'Brien" → studioobrien.com). This is compounding, relevant, and free. Confirm
  it's on every past + future client site.
- **Local orgs/sponsorships:** sponsor a local event/team/nonprofit → sponsor page
  link. Real community relevance.
- **Guest posts / podcasts:** local business podcasts, chamber blogs, industry
  sites — offer the expertise already written up on the blog.

## Measurement

- Track referring domains monthly (Search Console → Links, or the crawler).
- Watch local-pack + organic movement for the LocalIntel target keywords
  (Shelby NC web design, local SEO services Shelby NC, website design company
  Shelby NC, web development Shelby NC).
- Goal: a steady drip of relevant local links, not a spike. Spikes look manipulative.
