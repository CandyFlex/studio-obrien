## THE ENTRY FUNNEL — the "Founding Client" offer (first 3 businesses)

A real, professional small-business site at a founding rate, explicitly limited
to the first 3 local businesses, in exchange for a testimonial, a case study
(before/after data), and a referral.

- **Founding rate: $500–750** (recommended; your example was $500, blueprint
  says $500–800). Normally a Starter build is ~$1,500–2,500, so this is framed
  as **~$1,000 off, founding-only** — not your standard price.
- **Why it's still paid, not free:** a paying client (even a small amount) shows
  up, gives real feedback, and leaves a real testimonial. Skin in the game =
  a better case study. Free work gets treated as free.
- **Why "first 3, founding rate" framing matters:** it creates urgency, it
  protects your future pricing (nobody anchors to $500 as your real price), and
  it's honest — they genuinely are helping launch the studio locally.
- **What they get (overdeliver):** a full Starter-class site, GBP setup, on-page
  SEO, the "Site by Studio O'Brien" footer link, and your one-business-day care.

**The pitch is the free audit, not the discount.** Lead with their Local
Visibility Audit (SALES-ONBOARDING Stage 2) — the discount is what you offer
once they see the gap. Give first, then the easy yes.

## THE 3-CLIENT BENCHMARK PLAN (what makes the case studies real)

The reason to move fast on 3 clients: each becomes a measured before/after we
can prove — and we now own the tools to measure it (no fabrication needed).

For each Founding Client, capture BEFORE and AFTER:
- **Map-pack position** for their category (`maps-track.mjs`)
- **Reviews** count + rating vs. the local pack
- **Google visibility** / impressions (`gsc-queries.mjs` if they'll share GSC)
- **Site speed** + mobile (before = their old site or none)
- **The outcome** in their words (calls, bookings, "found us on Google")

That before/after is the case study, the testimonial prompt, and the proof that
justifies moving off founding rates. It also feeds the portfolio's case-study
template (already specified in AGENCY_BLUEPRINT.md §2).
