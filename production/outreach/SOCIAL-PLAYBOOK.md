# Studio O'Brien — Social + DM Cold-Approach Playbook

Section-10 companion to `OUTREACH-SYSTEM.md`. That file is doctrine; this
is the operational manual for every social surface, DM script, post format,
GBP move, and review ask. It inherits the master file's copy rules
(section 6), sequence protocol (section 8), and non-negotiables (section 15)
without restating them in full. Where this doc quotes copy meant to ship, it
follows the ship rules: no em dashes, no fabricated clients or outcomes,
concept work labeled concept, barstool test on every line.

**Voice (master section 6):** professional but down to earth. Every script
and post below reads like a capable neighbor who happens to do this for a
living, not an agency and not a comedian. The through-line is community
uplift: Jarred is a longtime entrepreneur who has started and tried plenty of
businesses himself, and he talks to other owners as a peer who has been in
their seat. Warmth comes from understanding their situation, not from
exclamation points. When a line could go polished or human, it goes human.

Built 2026-07-16. Market read: Shelby / Cleveland County, ~20k town, western
NC. This market skews heavily to Facebook. Plan the whole system around that
fact, not around what works in a metro.

Assets that actually exist and can be posted today:
- **Ben's Smokehouse** — BBQ concept build (label: concept)
- **Crownline Electric** — electrician concept build (label: concept)
- Additional concepts: HVAC, roofing, dental, bakery (label: concept)
- **Website-cost calculator** — free, interactive, on the site
- **The Field Guide** — the blog
- **Storefront Project** — free concept build for a real local business
- Real credentials: Top Rated on Upwork, 4.9 stars over 5 years, $100K+
  earned, moved home to Shelby. Frame as a capable studio built to grow, not
  a one-man shop. GBP is claimed and verified.

Time budget: ~2h batch on Wednesday + ~20 min/day + the posts themselves.

## 2. DM cold-approach scripts

All DMs obey master section 8: opener is short, one nudge at +7, channel
switch at +21, re-approach at +60 with NEW evidence only, "stop" is permanent.
Every DM logs to `CRM.md` with the channel noted (section 8 of this doc).
Post first, DM second week onward — never DM cold from a surface where the
studio has no visible activity.

### 2a. Facebook page Messenger (to a business page)

Etiquette: on a small-business page the **owner usually reads this
personally**. Keep it human, no link in the first message, no pitch. Send
from the studio page during business hours.

Opener variants (pick the one that fits what you actually found):

> Hey, I run a small web studio here in Shelby and I noticed something about
> how your shop shows up when folks search "bbq near me" around here. Happy
> to write it up and send it over if you want a look. One page, free, and I
> won't send anything till you say go.

> I've started a few businesses around here myself, so I pay attention to how
> local spots get found online. Put together a quick read on how yours shows
> up on Google. Want me to send it your way? No strings.

Nudge (+7, once):

> No rush on this at all. Still glad to send that read over whenever you have
> a minute. One page, free, nothing until you say the word.

Storefront-offer variant (Facebook-only business with no real website):

> I noticed y'all are getting found on Facebook with no website behind it,
> which honestly says a lot about your reputation. It's also a gap that's
> easier to close than most owners expect. Each round I build one website for
> a local business at no cost, and I'd be glad to build yours. Want to see
> what it could look like?

### 2b. Instagram DM

Etiquette: **an IG DM from an account with zero posts is an instant block.**
Posting comes first, full stop. Best for restaurants and visual businesses
already active on IG. Warm the target first: follow, react to a story,
comment something real once, THEN DM a few days later. No link in message one
(IG hides link-first DMs).

Opener:

> Been following the page and the food looks really good. I run a web studio
> here in town and I noticed something about how you show up when people
> search you on Google. Want me to send you a quick read on it? Free, one
> page, no pitch attached.

Nudge (+7):

> No rush at all, the offer stands whenever you have a minute. One page, free.

Storefront variant (IG-only, no site):

> You've built a genuinely strong brand on here with no website behind it
> yet. Each round I build one site for a local spot at no cost, and yours
> would be a great one to do. Okay if I mock something up and send it over?

### 2c. LinkedIn DM

Etiquette: professional services and **referral partners** only, not
tradesmen (they don't use it here). Realtors, accountants, attorneys,
insurance, clinics, regional agencies who might refer. Connect with a note,
don't pitch in the connect. Once connected, message. Slightly more formal,
still passes the barstool test.

Connect note:

> Fellow Shelby-area small business here. I run a web studio in town and I
> like knowing the people building things around here. Mind if I connect?

Opener (after connect):

> Thanks for connecting. Reason I reached out: I do website and local-search
> work for businesses around Cleveland County, and I noticed a couple of
> things about how your firm shows up on Google. Glad to write up a short read
> and send it over if it's useful. If not, no worries at all.

Referral-partner angle (when they serve the same owners we do):

> We probably talk to a lot of the same local owners. If you ever have a
> client who needs a real website, I'd be glad to be a name you can pass
> along, and I'll do the same for you. Might be worth a coffee sometime.

## 3. Posting system — repeatable formats

Six formats. Rotate them; never post the same format twice in a row. All
concept work labeled concept. No business ever named in a negative light.

### Format A — Build breakdown / before-after
**What:** A portfolio piece shown as problem to solution. Two-to-four images
or a short scrub. State plainly it's a concept build where true.
**Platforms:** FB page + IG feed (cross-post); IG reel version optional.
**Cadence:** 1/week. **Effort:** Low-med (assets exist).
Example caption:

> This is a concept build I made for a made-up BBQ spot called Ben's
> Smokehouse, just to work through a problem I see a lot with local
> restaurants. The menu is usually the whole thing, and nobody wants to
> pinch-zoom a PDF at a red light. So here the menu leads, the hours are one
> tap, and the phone number follows you down the page. Little stuff, but it's
> the difference between a website that works for you and one that just sits
> there.

### Format B — SERP anatomy mini-lesson
**What:** Teach what actually shows up when someone searches a local service.
Directories vs real businesses. Educational, never names a business
negatively.
**Platforms:** FB page + IG feed; strong LinkedIn crossover.
**Cadence:** 1 every 2 weeks. **Effort:** Med (one screenshot + plain writeup).
Example caption:

> Try searching "hvac shelby nc" on your phone right now. Count how many of
> the top results are actual local companies versus directory sites like
> Yelp, Angi, and Thumbtack that charge businesses to sit above them. In a lot
> of small NC towns it's three or four directories before you reach a real
> local business. If you're the owner, that space above the fold is worth
> paying attention to, and closing that gap is usually simpler than folks
> expect.

### Format C — Calculator promo
**What:** Point people at the free website-cost calculator. Frame around the
honest question owners actually ask.
**Platforms:** FB page + IG feed + Stories + GBP.
**Cadence:** 1 every 2-3 weeks (don't wear it out). **Effort:** Low.
Example caption:

> "How much does a website actually cost" is one of the hardest questions to
> get a straight answer on. As someone who has had to budget for this stuff
> myself, I built a calculator that gives you a real range in about a minute.
> No email required, no sales call attached. Link's in the bio. Use it even if
> we never talk, that's genuinely fine.

### Format D — Field Guide amplification
**What:** Pull one sharp idea from a blog post, post it as a standalone
thought, link to the full article.
**Platforms:** FB page + LinkedIn (best fit) + IG Stories link.
**Cadence:** 1/week when a fresh or evergreen post is available. **Effort:** Low.
Example caption:

> New one in the Field Guide on why your Google Business Profile probably
> matters more than your website when it comes to getting found locally. Short
> version: the map pack sits above every website result, and most owners have
> never really set theirs up. It's free and it's worth an afternoon. Full
> breakdown on the site.

### Format E — Storefront Project spotlight / nomination call
**What:** Announce or re-announce the free concept build. Ask for nominations.
Once #1 ships, this becomes a real spotlight (Phase 2).
**Platforms:** FB page + groups (only on group promo-days), IG, GBP, Nextdoor.
**Cadence:** 1 every 2-3 weeks until a slot fills, then spotlight the result.
**Effort:** Low.
Example caption:

> Each round I build one website for a local business at no cost, no catch.
> I've started enough businesses of my own around here to know how far a good
> reputation can carry you and how much a rough website can hold you back.
> Know a Cleveland County shop that's great at what they do but has a weak or
> missing website? Tag them or send me a name and I'll pick one.

### Format F — Behind-the-build / workshop shot
**What:** Process glimpse. Screen mid-build, a sketch, a before/after tweak, a
"here's what I'm working on today." Humanizes the studio.
**Platforms:** IG Stories + FB page; casual.
**Cadence:** 1-2/week, low stakes. **Effort:** Very low.
Example caption:

> Tuesday at the desk, reworking a menu section so it loads before the big
> photo does. Sounds small, but a hungry person deciding where to eat won't
> wait three seconds for a page to catch up, and they shouldn't have to.

## 5. Facebook group participation doctrine

The single fastest way to burn this market is to pitch in a group. Master
section 15: no pitching in Facebook groups. Participation builds the warm
field; DMs (section 2) harvest it.

**Finding + vetting groups.** Do NOT assume group names. Search Facebook for
these terms and vet each result before joining:
- "Shelby NC" / "Cleveland County NC"
- "Shelby buy sell trade" / "Cleveland County buy sell trade"
- "Shelby NC community" / "Kings Mountain" / "Boiling Springs NC"
- "Cleveland County small business" / "Shelby NC business owners"
- "[town] recommendations" / "[town] happenings"

Vet on: member count (bigger + local = better), post activity (dead groups
waste time), admin rules (read the pinned rules and About section for
self-promo policy and any promo-day), and whether real local owners actually
post there vs bots and yard-sale spam.

**Give-first rules:**
- Default is zero self-promo. You are a helpful local, not a vendor.
- Answer questions in your lane (websites, Google, getting found online)
  with a genuinely useful reply and no link and no "DM me." Being right in
  public is the entire strategy.
- Aim for a give-to-ask ratio you'd never be embarrassed by. In practice:
  many helpful comments, near-zero asks.

**Handling "anyone know a website person" threads:**
- These are gold and also a trap. Do not drop a sales pitch.
- Best move: a short, human, useful reply that helps whether or not they pick
  you. Example that ships:

  > Happy to point you the right direction. One thing worth knowing: make sure
  > whoever builds it also sets up your Google Business Profile, because for
  > local that's often what actually gets you found. I do this here in town if
  > you ever want to talk it through, but either way you're on the right track.

- Even better when someone else can vouch: let the thread recommend you. If a
  past client is in the group, a tag from them beats anything you say.
- Then take it to DM only if they engage. Log the thread as demand evidence
  in CRM regardless.

**When a post is actually allowed:**
- Only on a group's explicit promo-day, and only if the rules permit it, and
  even then rarely (a real Storefront nomination call, Format E, fits best).
- One promo post per group per promo-cycle, maximum. Read the room.
- If in doubt, don't. One "this guy spams" comment in a 20k town costs more
  than a hundred posts earn (master section 1).

## 6. GBP playbook

GBP is the highest-ROI local surface. It sits above website results in the
map pack. Already claimed and verified.

**Weekly post types (rotate, 1/week minimum):**
- **What's New** — a build breakdown or Field Guide idea (Format A/D reused).
- **Offer** — the free calculator or the Storefront Project (no fake
  scarcity, master section 15; "each round I build one free site" is true,
  "2 slots left today" is not).
- **Update** — a real studio note (new concept shipped, new article).
Each post: one clear image, 2-3 plain sentences, one call to action, link to
the relevant page (calculator, Field Guide, contact).

**Categories + services hygiene:**
- Primary category: Website Designer. Secondary: Marketing Agency (per
  ROADMAP Phase 1).
- Services list filled with plain-language services people search: website
  design, website redesign, local SEO, Google Business Profile setup,
  restaurant websites, small business websites.
- Confirm NAP (name/address/phone) matches the site and every citation
  exactly. Inconsistent NAP quietly kills map-pack ranking.
- Service area set to Cleveland County + adjacent towns.

**Q&A seeding (honest):**
- Owners are allowed to ask and answer their own GBP questions. Seed the ones
  real prospects actually ask, answered straight:
  - "How much does a website cost?" — point to the honest range + the
    calculator, no dodge.
  - "Do you work with businesses outside Shelby?" — yes, and say so.
  - "Do you set up Google Business Profiles too?" — yes, and why it matters.
- Never fake a testimonial-style question. Q&A is information, not proof
  laundering.

**Photo cadence:**
- Add photos steadily, not all at once. A few per month: build screenshots,
  behind-the-build shots, the workspace. Fresh photos are a freshness signal.
- Real images only. No stock pretending to be the studio.

## 7. Reviews engine

Reviews power both the map pack and the future testimonials section (ROADMAP
Phase 1 + 2). This is outreach (master section 10). Hard rules first because
they're absolute.

**Hard rules (Google policy + master section 15):**
- No incentives. Never offer anything for a review. Ever.
- No gating (don't screen for happy customers before asking). Ask everyone
  worth asking, unconditionally.
- Never fabricate a review or write one for someone.
- One review ask per week, maximum (Friday, per master section 13).

**Who to ask:** real past and current clients. Upwork-era clients count and
should be asked first because they're real relationships with real outcomes.
Start with the strongest relationships.

**The ask (personal, one-to-one, with the direct Google review link):**

> Hey [Name], it was genuinely good working with you on [project]. If you have
> a couple of minutes, a short Google review would mean a lot to me. It really
> helps people around here find the studio and decide to give it a shot.
> Here's the direct link so it's quick: [review link]. No worries at all if
> you're slammed, I know how it goes.

- Send it where you actually know them (email, the platform you worked on,
  text if that's your relationship). Personal, not a blast.
- Space asks one per week so it never feels like a campaign.

**When a review lands:**
1. Reply to it within a day or two. Specific, warm, human, no template.
   Example: "Thanks [Name], the [specific thing] on that project was a blast
   to build. Glad it's working for you."
2. With permission, repurpose it as a social post (a clean quote card + a
   line of context) and, once the testimonials section is restored, as a real
   on-site testimonial.
3. Log it in CRM (reviews-gained metric, section 8).
- Negative or lukewarm review: reply calmly and publicly, fix what's fixable,
  never argue. One graceful reply beats ten defensive ones.

## 8. Tracking hooks

Everything social feeds the same CRM and metrics discipline as email. Nothing
lives in memory (master section 4).

**DM logging — every DM logs to `CRM.md`, same as email**, with a channel
column so FB / IG / LinkedIn DMs sit in the one pipeline alongside email
touches. Log at minimum: target, channel, route (A / B1 / B2), step + date
sent, response, next-touch date. The sequence protocol (master section 8)
governs DMs identically to email: opener → +7 nudge → +21 channel switch →
+60 new-evidence re-approach → +120 dormant; "stop" is permanent and logged
same day.

**Post metrics — reviewed Fridays** (master section 13/14 review block).
Record per post and in weekly totals:

| Metric | Where | Why it matters |
|---|---|---|
| Reach / impressions | FB + IG insights | Is the warm field growing? |
| Reactions / comments | FB + IG | Which formats actually land (double the winners) |
| Profile clicks | FB + IG | Warm-field to interest conversion |
| DMs started (inbound) | FB + IG | The point of posting: harvest signal |
| Link clicks (calculator / Field Guide) | GBP + IG bio + GA4 | On-site intent from social |
| GBP post views + calls/clicks | GBP insights | Map-pack engine health |
| Reviews gained | GBP | The compounding proof asset |

**Cadence discipline:** log weekly totals every Friday. Monthly, kill or fix
the worst-performing format and double the best (master section 14). Do not
chase vanity reach; the funnel metric is DMs started and inbound inquiries
(GA4 `generate_lead`), not follower count.

**Approval gate (master section 15):** Claude drafts posts and DMs; Jarred
personally approves the week's batch and sends everything. The from-line is
always a real person.
