# PART 2 — PRICING SYSTEMS (pick one to build on)

### System A — "Pick-2 + Add-Ons" (your stated idea)
- Monthly **base includes any 2 modules** [SET base $].
- Each additional module = **flat add-on** [SET per-module $].
- Swap modules anytime (pivot).
- **Pros:** dead simple, predictable, easy to pitch. **Cons:** flat add-on can
  feel expensive fast; no built-in reward for going deep.

### System B — "Tiered Module Packs"
- **Starter** = 2 modules · **Growth** = 4 · **Pro** = 6+ — each a set monthly
  [SET], and the per-module cost drops as the tier rises (volume discount baked
  into the tier).
- **Pros:** clean choice (pick a tier), volume discount automatic, upsell path
  obvious. **Cons:** less granular; some clients want exactly 3.

### System C — "Base + Pick-2, then Bundled Add-On Steps" (my recommendation)
- Base (hero + care) **includes 2 modules of their choice** [SET base $].
- Add modules in **steps that get cheaper per module**: +1–2 modules at rate X,
  +3–4 at rate Y (lower), 5+ at rate Z (lowest). [SET the curve]
- Fully swappable; we re-evaluate the mix each quarter and pivot with them.
- **Pros:** your pick-2 idea + your volume-discount idea + pivot flexibility, in
  one. **Cons:** one more sentence to explain than A.

*(Optional System D — Credits: monthly buys N credits, modules cost credits by
effort, reallocate monthly. Most flexible, hardest to explain — noted, not
recommended unless you love it.)*

**Different bands by pipeline** applies on top of whichever system: a
grant/community client pays a lower per-module rate than a commercial client
(see Part 3).

# PART 3 — "Monthly bands," plainly (the question you didn't get)
"Band" just = which price level applies. Same module, different price depending
on the pipeline the client came in through:
- **Grant band** (nominated) — lowest per-module rate.
- **Referral band** (referred by a nominee) — middle.
- **Commercial band** (direct) — full rate.
So "Review Generation" might be, say, $X grant / $1.5X referral / $2X commercial
[SET]. **The question is just: do grant clients pay less per module than
commercial ones, and by how much?** (My rec: yes — same discount logic as the
build price, so the whole pipeline stays consistent.)
