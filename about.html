Coming home to build

I'm Jarred O'Brien, and I've come home to Shelby to build a studio worth my family's name. After years of design and development work for clients across the country, I moved back to Cleveland County to plant Studio O'Brien right here and build for the businesses around me.

I'm not new to this. I spent five years as a Top Rated designer and developer on Upwork, holding a 4.9-star rating and delivering more than $100,000 in work across custom websites, brand and graphic design, illustration, and SEO for all kinds of businesses. That's the range of a full agency, held to the standard of someone who has done it at a high level for years. What is new is where I am pointing it: at my own community.

Studio O'Brien is founder-led and built to grow. The plan is to make it the studio this region reaches for, adding to the team and the work as we go, and reinvesting in the area the whole way. You are not hiring a hobby. You are hiring a studio that intends to be here for the long run.

The standard behind every project

Every project the studio takes on is built to a professional standard, from the first plan to the day it launches. I have delivered for demanding clients who expected agency-quality work, and local businesses get held to exactly that bar. Nothing gets phoned in because a project is small or close to home.

We build in real code rather than on a page-builder platform, and that choice is for your benefit more than ours. It keeps your site fast, keeps it yours, and keeps it flexible enough to grow alongside your business. No monthly platform rent, no template shared with thousands of other companies, no forced rebuild the week a plugin stops working.

Just as important, I try to make the whole process easy on you. A lot of owners arrive a little worn out by past web experiences and unsure what they even need. Figuring that out with you, then carrying the technical weight so you do not have to, is the part of this work I care about most.

What the studio does

Studio O'Brien covers the whole picture, the way an agency would, without the agency overhead:

  • Web design and development: fast, custom-built, mobile-first sites, and redesigns that turn visitors into calls.
  • Local SEO and Google Business Profile: getting you found in the map results, even in a competitive small market.
  • Brand and graphic design: logos, visual identity, and the details that make a business look as established as it is.
  • Technical SEO and accessibility: migrations, structured data, page speed, and ADA/WCAG done properly.

Who we build for

The studio does its best work for ambitious local businesses. Restaurants, shops, trades, studios, and professional practices that are ready to look as good online as they already are in person. If you take your business seriously, we will get along.

I will also be straight with you about fit, because it saves us both time. If you want the cheapest possible template or a build run by committee, there are better matches out there. But if you want work that raises how your business is seen, and a partner who tells you the truth, that is exactly what Studio O'Brien is for.

What you walk away with

When your project ships, it is yours. All of it: the code, the content, the domain, the accounts. Studio O'Brien does not lock your website inside a platform only we can log into, and there is no subscription that quietly disables your design the month you stop paying. If you ever move on, you take everything with you. I wrote a full breakdown of what owning your website actually means, because too many owners learn the hard way that they never really did.

Why I write The Field Guide

Most small-business web advice is either vague agency filler or SEO copy written to rank instead of help. I started The Field Guide because the owners I work with kept asking the same good questions. Does my Google profile really matter? Is my slow site quietly costing me sales? Are these ADA lawsuit emails real? They deserved straight answers with the numbers to back them up.

So every article starts from a real question and leans on cited data. When I tell you Google Business Profile signals drive about a third of your local ranking, there is a source on that number. When it is my own read rather than a study, I say so. You can see exactly how each piece gets researched and checked on the editorial standards page.
