# Positioning & Voice Foundation

The source of truth for who Studio O'Brien is and how it sounds. Every
site section, portfolio caption, blog post, and outreach message gets
checked against THIS. Written 2026-07-17 with Jarred, to resolve a
two-voice conflict (the old PRODUCT.md "bold/polarizing" brand voice vs.
the warm "capable neighbor" outreach voice). This doc wins; where
PRODUCT.md still says "polarizing beats safe," PRODUCT.md is the one that
needs reconciling (see "What this changes").

---

## The spine (one line)

The local guide who keeps small businesses found and growing as the
digital world speeds up.

## What you sell (the engine)

Outcomes, not design. Getting found on Google. Getting called and
visited. Looking legit enough to be trusted. The website is just how
those get delivered — the product is the result, not the pixels.

## Why you do it (the soul)

Tech and AI are accelerating every year, and that widens the gap for
small businesses in rural counties like ours. Jarred went out, learned
this craft at a professional level, and came home to be the bridge — so
the businesses in his own backyard keep up instead of getting left behind.

## Who it's for

The local owner who measures a website by customers, not by how it looks
in a design gallery. Primary buyer = the skeptical WNC owner (the Jan's
Restaurant in Fallston type). The design-savvy national visitor is
welcome and converts on the strength of the work, but the VOICE is tuned
for the local owner. When the two audiences want opposite things, the
local owner wins.

## How you sound (the voice)

- **Friendly neighbor who happens to do this at a pro level.** Peer to
  peer, one owner to another. Warmth comes from understanding their
  situation, not from exclamation points or jokes.
- **Bold in the work you show, warm in the words you say.** The boldness
  lives in the visuals, the type, and the portfolio. The words stay
  human. This is how the site and a cold email sound like the same person.
- **Plain-spoken, never dumbed-down.** When a precise (even technical)
  word is the right word, use it — and take ten seconds to teach it and
  why it matters to them. Educating a little IS the mission. Lift people
  up to the word; never talk over them (agency-speak) or beneath them
  (baby-talk).
- **Professional and precise.** Clear enough to follow, exact enough to
  trust. Certain words carry real meaning; keep them, explain them.
- **The unbreakable rule: never condescending.** The frame is always
  "the pace got unfair — you shouldn't have to run your business AND
  become a tech expert; that part's my job." The world sped up; it is
  never "you're behind."

### The three-register calibration (settle every word choice with this)

- ❌ Agency (hides meaning): "We leverage SEO to optimize your digital footprint."
- ❌ Dumbed-down (talks beneath): "SEO is just Google stuff, don't worry about it."
- ✅ You (right word + quick teach): "SEO just means showing up when
  someone nearby searches for what you sell. Here's the part that moves
  the needle for a shop like yours..."

### Carry-over hard rules (from existing doctrine, still in force)

- No em dashes in anything that ships to a prospect or onto the site. Hyphens only.
- No fabricated clients, testimonials, stats, or outcomes. Proof slots
  stay empty until real. Concept work is labeled concept work.
- Specificity is voice: real towns, real queries, real numbers, real
  tools by name. "When someone's AC dies," not "in today's digital landscape."

## The promise (in your voice)

"You run your business. I'll keep it visible."

   story, in the neighbor voice, not design adjectives.
4. **Blog / Field Guide** — the "teach a little" register is the native
   voice for this content; align existing pieces on the byline retrofit pass.
5. **Outreach copy** — already close (voice-calibration memory). Confirm
   the Gap Read + openers pass the three-register test and the
   never-condescending rule.
