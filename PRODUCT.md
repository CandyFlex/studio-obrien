# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

**Primary:** independent business owners across western North Carolina — Cleveland, Gaston, Lincoln, Catawba, Rutherford counties and surrounding areas. Restaurants, retail, hospitality, professional services, creative work. Local SEO and location pages anchor this audience.

**Secondary:** business owners outside the region (anywhere in the Carolinas, the US, beyond) who find the site through portfolio, content, or referral. The site does not turn them away — it converts them on the strength of the work, not on geography.

Reading context: skeptical of agency speak. Comparing options (templates, freelancers, agencies). The local audience is often less screen-literate but trusts plain language and visible craft. The national audience is often more design-savvy and wants to see a POV worth hiring for.

## Product Purpose

Maximize qualified contact-form submissions. The homepage and supporting pages exist to take a visitor from "evaluating options" to "I want to start a project with this person specifically." Conversion volume is the metric — not raw traffic, not vanity reputation, not nurture-cycle engagement.

Every section earns its place by either (a) demonstrating the work, (b) reducing friction on the next step, or (c) building enough specific belief that the visitor takes the next step. Sections that do none of those should be cut.

## Brand Personality

**Bold. Opinionated. Distinctive.**

A site that has something to say and isn't worried about being polarizing. Big typography, expressive moves, a willingness to commit to a POV when most competitors hedge. The opposite of "we make beautiful websites for forward-thinking brands."

Voice in copy: direct, specific, occasionally sharp. Names things by name. Has favorites. Doesn't try to sound like an agency.

A polarizing site is doing its job: the visitors it loses are the wrong-fit ones, and the visitors it keeps are walking in pre-sold.

## Anti-references

Three lanes this site must NOT occupy:

1. **Template SaaS marketing.** Gradient hero → three-feature cards → testimonial wall → pricing table. The Squarespace-template look. If a section's structure could be a Webflow preset, redesign it.
2. **Generic agency site.** Hero-metric template, identical case-card grids, "we craft digital experiences across industries" copy. The same site in 10,000 cities.
3. **Editorial-typographic AI lane.** Display serif (Cormorant / Fraunces / Newsreader) + small uppercase tracked eyebrows + ruled separators + monochrome restraint. The saturated 2025–26 portfolio look — every Stripe-adjacent indie studio has landed here. Specifically banned because the current draft was drifting toward it.

Permitted but watched: **dark-mode treatment.** The site is dark and uses a mint-green accent. That's an identity decision, not a problem — but it must NOT collapse into navy-and-electric-blue SaaS / glassmorphism / "AI workflow tool" SaaS. Distinctive dark, not generic dark.

## Design Principles

1. **Have a POV, on every section.** Don't present neutrally. Every block should make a claim, recommend a path, or reveal a preference. Neutrality is the agency-site default.
2. **Big type carries the brand.** Display typography is the loudest brand carrier on the page. Commit to scale, weight contrast, and confident letter-spacing. Timid type = timid brand.
3. **Show, don't say.** The work is the proof. Portfolio sections, real screenshots, real quotes, real outcomes outweigh any copy describing craft.
4. **Specific over general.** Real town names. Real client names. Real numbers. Real tools by name. Specificity is the single biggest tell of a human-built page vs an AI-built one.
5. **Conversion is the design.** The contact CTA is not a separate concern from the brand. Make the next step obvious, reduce friction, repeat the path. A bold site that buries its CTA fails its own brief.
6. **Polarizing beats safe.** If the design's worst trait is "it's a lot for some people," that's working. If its worst trait is "I've seen this 50 times," start over.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA target. Body text ≥4.5:1 contrast. Reduced-motion fallbacks on every animation. Tap targets ≥44px on mobile. Forms labeled, alt text describes the project not the filename. Nothing beyond standard.
