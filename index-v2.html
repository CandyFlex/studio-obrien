Questions, answered straight.

The things people ask before they reach out. No fine print.

How much does a custom website cost?

Every project is scoped to what the site actually needs to do, so there’s no fixed package price. Most custom sites start in the low four figures and scale with page count, copywriting, and functionality.

Want a number now? Get an instant, no-email ballpark with the website cost calculator.

How long does a website take to build?

Most sites go from kickoff to launch in about three to six weeks. The biggest variable is how ready your content is: copy and photos. I help with both, so a slow start doesn’t stall the build.

Do you only work with businesses in Shelby?

No. I’m based in Shelby and know Cleveland, Gaston, Lincoln, Rutherford, and Catawba counties well, but I work with independent businesses anywhere in the Carolinas and beyond. The work travels; the local roots are a bonus, not a limit.

Who owns the website when it’s finished?

You do, completely. Full source code and ownership transfer to you at launch. No proprietary builder lock-in, no platform you have to keep renting to stay online.

Do you help with Google and getting found locally?

Yes. On-page SEO and Google Business Profile setup are built into the work, especially for local businesses. Showing up when someone in your town searches for what you do is the whole point of the site.

What if I don’t have a website or any content yet?

That’s common, and it’s fine. I help with structure, copywriting, and photo direction from scratch. Businesses with nothing online yet are exactly who the Storefront Project is built for.
