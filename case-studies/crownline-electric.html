We build the standard first.

Search for an electrician in most towns and every result looks the same: a stock photo of a breaker panel, a phone number buried three clicks deep, and a template doing the least work it can. The businesses behind those sites are often the best crews in the county. Their websites just don’t say so.

We think the trades deserve better storefronts, so we don’t wait to be hired. We design and code a complete site for one trade at a time, at the standard we’d defend to any client. Then we nominate a shortlist of local businesses whose work outclasses their current website and send them this page.

Crownline is the electrical build. It isn’t a mockup waiting on a client, and nothing on it is filler. It’s finished work, and one business gets to make it theirs.

What’s in the build.

Call-first layout

A tap-to-call button stays within reach in every section, because customers with a dead outlet phone, they don’t fill out forms.

Service pages built for search

Each core service gets its own page, structured the way Google matches “electrician near me” and “panel upgrade” searches.

Review proof up front

A Google-reviews block sits in the first screen, doing the trust work before a visitor reads a single paragraph.

Booking built in

An appointment button rides alongside the call button, for the planners who’d rather schedule than dial.

Mobile-first build

Designed at phone width before desktop. Most urgent searches happen in a parking lot, not at a desk, and the layout respects that.

Hand-written code

No WordPress, no page builder, no stack of plugins waiting to break or slow the site down. Just clean pages that load fast on a weak signal.

Local SEO structure

Clean headings, meta tags, and local-business schema, so the site is legible to Google from the day it goes live.

Yours outright

When it launches under your name, you own the site. No platform lock-in and no retainer required to keep it alive.

Why it looks like this.

One color, owned.

A single amber runs from the wordmark to the buttons to the hover states. On a results page full of gray competitors, the brand reads from across the room.

Type does the selling.

Heavy, confident headings state the offer plainly. No thin script over a dimmed stock photo, which is the template default in this trade.

Proof over promises.

Reviews, licensing, and warranty badges sit high on the page. The layout walks a visitor from problem to proof to phone in one pass.

Claimed to launched in about a week.

The design and engineering are already done. Claiming the build means rebuilding it around your business, and that part moves fast.
