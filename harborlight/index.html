How adoption works

Three steps, usually one to two weeks start to finish.

  1. Send an inquiry

    Use the form below or call the line. Tell us who you're interested in and a little about your household. We reply within a day.

  2. Meet and greet

    Visit by appointment, or meet the animal at its foster home. Bring current pets if the match needs testing. No commitment at this stage.

  3. Home check and take-home

    A volunteer does a short home visit, you sign the adoption agreement, and your new family member comes home with records, food to start, and our 24-hour support line.

Foster or volunteer

We have no facility by design. The whole organization is people who share their homes and their time.

Foster a rescue

Every animal we accept sleeps in a foster home the same night, so our capacity is exactly the number of homes we can call. We currently have 61. We need 100.

  • We cover everything: food, supplies, and all veterinary care.
  • Average stay is three weeks. You choose the species, size, and timing.
  • Adopting your foster is allowed and happens often. 74 times so far.

Volunteer with us

Not everyone can foster. The rescue also runs on drivers, photographers, event hands, and people willing to answer a phone at odd hours.

  • Transport: vet runs and adoption-day drives, mostly within the county.
  • Photography and bios: good photos get animals adopted measurably faster.
  • Home visits and events: a few hours a month, scheduled around you.

Common questions

Can I visit the animals?

Yes, by appointment. Because our animals live in foster homes rather than a shelter, we schedule meetings at the Skiff Lane house or at the foster home, whichever suits the animal better. Send an inquiry or call the line to set one up.

What does the adoption fee include?

Spay or neuter surgery, age-appropriate vaccinations, deworming, flea treatment, and a registered microchip. Dogs also go home with a collar, tag, and starter food. The fee covers roughly half of what we actually spend per animal.

How long does adoption take?

Usually one to two weeks: inquiry, a meet and greet, then a short home visit before take-home day. If an animal has multiple applicants, the foster family helps pick the best match rather than first-come-first-served.

I need to surrender an animal. Can you help?

Call the 24-hour line first. We accept surrenders as foster space allows and prioritize emergencies. If we're full, we'll help you find alternatives; we would rather help you rehome safely than have an animal abandoned.

Does fostering cost me anything?

No. We supply food, crates, litter, and all veterinary care, and we reimburse mileage for vet runs. You provide the home and the attention. The average foster stay is about three weeks.

Are donations tax-deductible?

Yes. Harborlight is a registered 501(c)(3) nonprofit (EIN 88-0000000) and every donation receives a receipt. We publish a full financial summary each January; 92 cents of every dollar goes directly to animal care.
